Thank you for installing YT Music Organizer. Let us show you how to get started.

How Does It Work?

1

Open a Playlist

Go to YouTube Music and open any of your own editable playlists.

2

Click "Organize"

A button will appear at the bottom-right corner of the screen. Click it to open the Sort Builder.

3

Pick Your Criteria

Choose one or two sorting criteria. The extension will rearrange your playlist automatically.

Sorting Criteria

Choose how to reorganize your playlist. You can pick up to two criteria that combine into powerful fusions:

Artist Name

Groups all songs by artist in alphabetical order (A-Z).

Album (Year/Order)

Older albums come first. Songs stay in the original album track order.

Popularity

Most famous songs at the top, based on Last.fm listeners or YouTube views.

My Scrobbles

Songs you listened to the most on Last.fm come first. Requires a Last.fm account.

Track Title

Songs in alphabetical order by title (A-Z).

Duration

Shortest songs first, longest songs last.

True Shuffle

Completely randomizes your playlist order.

The Magic Fusions

Combine two criteria to unlock special sorting modes:

  • Hall of Fame

    Artist A-Z + Popularity. Greatest masterpieces first!

  • Classic Discography

    Artist A-Z + Album chronological. Dive into each artist's career.

  • My Favorite Albums

    Scrobbles + Album. Your most-listened albums from Last.fm come first.

  • Band's Greatest Hits

    Artist A-Z + Popularity. Biggest hits play first.

  • Fan Club

    Artist A-Z + Scrobbles. Your most-played songs per band.

  • Express Session

    Duration + Album. Shortest albums first — perfect for quick sessions.

Last.fm Setup

Connect your Last.fm account to unlock Scrobble-based sorting and better Popularity accuracy.

Why connect Last.fm?

  • Enables "My Scrobbles" sorting — organize by what YOU actually listen to
  • Improves Popularity accuracy with Last.fm listener data
  • Unlocks fusion modes: Fan Club, My Top Artists, My Favorite Albums

How to get your API Key

  1. Go to the Last.fm API page (link below)
  2. Log in or create a free Last.fm account
  3. Fill in any name for "Application Name" (e.g. "YTM Organizer")
  4. Copy the "API Key" that appears on the next page
